Colonized in the 2000s by both the Americans and Soviets, `Mercury` is known for little more than hosting an extensive mining operation. A significant portion of the world has been processed by the current day, leading to a clear "missing chunk" of the planet, visible even from deep space.

There is little of interest on the surface. One of the few notable regions is `ZONA D-88`, a mining site designated by the Union sometime in the 2000s. It is home to a critical Sunset Research Initiative outpost and sarcophagus, the contents of which were a mystery to the rest of the human sphere. Old tales told by miners spoke of a strange melted city made of gold...
